Why RDM matters

  • Data is a research output, not just a by-product
  • Ensures reproducibility and transparency
  • Facilitates collaboration across disciplines and institutions
  • Supports FAIR Principles
  • Aligns with CARE Principles for Indigenous data

FAIR: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable

CARE: Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics

What is “data”?

  • Broad definition: recorded information supporting research findings
  • Data can be qualitative or quantitative
  • Useful lens: the 5 V’s of Big Data

ArcticNet

  • The ADMP applies to all data generated wholly or partially from ArcticNet–funded research, viewing it as a public good, while respecting privacy, ethics, and intellectual property.
  • Key Objectives:
    • Implement national/international best practices in data stewardship
    • Maximize accessibility, avoid duplication, promote reuse—especially for Inuit, First Nations and Métis communities
    • Uphold FAIRness, encourage interdisciplinary sharing and responsible handling of sensitive data
    • Support data sovereignty: respect CARE principles (and distinctions-based guidance like OCAP®, NISR) for Indigenous data governance

Guiding Principles

Principle Description
Open Yet Protected Data & metadata should be as open as possible, and as closed as necessary (e.g. embargoes, privacy, confidentiality)
Citable & Preserved ArcticNet data must be citable, publishable, and preserved, with interoperability and links across platforms
Infrastructure & Support Encourage use of existing interoperable systems; support researchers via training, resources, and outreach

ArcticNet Data Management Policy

ArcticNet

Requirements for Research Projects

  • Mandatory DMP: Projects must create and continually update a Data Management Plan covering lifecycle stages, responsibilities, metadata, ethics, etc.
  • Metadata & Standards: Use accepted disciplinary metadata standards (e.g., ISO, FGDC, Dublin Core); submit rich metadata early; failure to do so may delay funding releasesArcticNet-Data-Management-Policy-ADMP_Approved-March-2025.pdf)
  • Storage & Preservation: Researchers choose certified repositories (e.g., Nordicana-D, Polar Data Catalogue, GBIF, OBIS) and plan for long-term archival using preservation-friendly formatsArcticNet-Data-Management-Policy-ADMP_Approved-March-2025.pdf)
  • Access & Sharing: Data must be published openly when appropriate; exceptions include sensitive or Indigenous data, which must be justified in the DMP
  • Sensitive & Indigenous Data: Follow anonymization, privacy protections, and engage Indigenous communities fully—respect CARE, OCAP®, NISR protocols
  • Monitoring & Reporting: Annual progress reports must include metadata links and any data-related incidents; researchers must notify and report security breaches within 24 hours

Data Management Plan – 30min

Importance

A data management plan, or DMP, is a formal document that outlines how data will be handled during and after a research project. Many funding agencies, especially government sources, require a DMP as part of their application processes.

  • https://datamanagement.hms.harvard.edu/plan-design/data-management-plans

  • https://dmp-pgd.ca/public_templates

  • McGill:

    • RDM https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jm7qIkrL3wM
    • DMP https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p_JzQxxC4ts

Data collection

  • Protocols
  • Types of data (e.g., observational, model outputs, images)
  • Formats and estimated volume
  • Structure and naming conventions

Documentation and Metadata

  • Standards used (e.g., ISO 19115, Darwin Core)
  • Tools or platforms used for metadata creation (e.g., Polar Data Catalog)
  • Timing of metadata creation (start early, update as needed)
  • ArcticNet will provide a template for PDC metadata submission

Storage, Backup, Preservation

  • How data will be stored
  • How will you ensure that data will be preserved
  • V value

Sharing and Reuse

  • Who will access the data and when (open, embargoed, restricted)
  • How data will be cited (DOIs or other persistent IDs)
  • Licensing terms (e.g., CC-BY, custom agreements)

Legal & Ethical Considerations

  • Privacy concerns, ethics approvals
  • Indigenous data sovereignty and OCAP/CARE principles
  • Embargo periods, licensing, or access restrictions
